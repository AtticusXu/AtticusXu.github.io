About Me

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Applied Mathematics at The Ohio State University, working under the supervision of Prof. Dongbin Xiu. Born in Beijing, China in 1998, I have been passionate about mathematics and computational sciences from an early age.

My research focuses on developing innovative machine learning methods for scientific computing, particularly in the areas of digital twin technology, function approximation using neural networks, and modeling of stochastic dynamical systems. I am interested in bridging the gap between theoretical mathematics and practical applications in computational fluid dynamics and data-driven modeling.

Research Interests

Research Areas

Digital Twin Technology

Developing targeted digital twin systems via flow map learning with applications to fluid dynamics and real-time system monitoring.

Neural Network Function Approximation

Designing Chebyshev feature neural networks for accurate function approximation and exploring novel architectural approaches.

Stochastic Dynamical Systems

Modeling unknown stochastic dynamical systems via autoencoder architectures and developing data-driven approaches for incomplete data scenarios.
